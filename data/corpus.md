EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Following the intentions of Association of Insurers in Rwanda (ASSAR) to further
strengthen the Insurance Market in Rwanda and following the review of the non-motor
rates as recommended by the Regulator; the Excom of ASSAR after several
deliberations on the need for this publication adopted a resolution to commission the
production of the publication.
The Aims and Objectives of the publication have been detailed above and it is
envisaged that the publication will go a long way in guiding Underwriters in the
respective insurance companies in the consideration and pricing of General Insurance
Risks.
It is hoped that with time, some of the indices which inform the pricing of risks such as
the declaration of previous loss ratios, commissioning and compliance with Survey
recommendations etc will become common knowledge and be followed by insurance
consumers as well as the individual insurance companies in the market and thereby
eliminate any untoward practices in the engagement of risks which hitherto may have
been due to lack of standardisation and uniformity in the market.
It is to be understood that the need for this publication became inevitable following
the aftermath of the study commissioned by ASSAR on the state of the health of the
insurance industry in Rwanda and recommendations from the study showed clearly
that efforts needed to be put in place for a uniform practice in the industry so as to
ensure a veritable future for the industry as well as enhance service to insurance
consumers in the market.
The preceding study was carried out by a renowned firm of Actuaries operating under
the name ACTSERV and based in Kenya whilst the production of the necessary resultant
Industry Operating Manuals as well as the pricing manual and which were then
consigned to ASSAR Technical Committee jointly with industry underwriter and a well-
known industry consultancy firm by the name MATICS Ltd.

Introduction
Consequent upon the decision by ASSAR to commission the production of the relevant
industry operating Manuals and the Pricing Manual; it was decided that 3 major
Operational Manuals needed to be produced to guide professional practice in the
industry.
The manuals are:
 UNDERWRITING MANUAL
 GENERAL BUSINESS PRICING MANUAL and
 CLAIMS MANAGEMENT MANUAL
All the manuals in their respective rights are to provide the necessary guidelines to
industry practitioners in the respective insurance companies with a view to achieving
a reasonable level of uniformity in the market and thereby eliminate any undue
favours or undue practice in the handling and processing of a new business or in the
handling of a reported claim.
It was envisaged that where all practitioners adopt a uniform process modality; each
operating company is then expected to grow their businesses by way of CUSTOMER
SERVICE.
Insurance consumers, be it Corporate or individual are then expected to experience
the same procedures irrespective of the company they chose to patronise.
However, consumers will over time notice the difference in service delivery from the
different insurers and develop customer loyalty on that basis.
It is also believed that such a development will engender stability and growth in the
market and provide a win - win situation to all concerned
Finally, Insurance Companies operating in the market would have themselves agreed
to additional operating rules which would have complemented the Industry Regulator
(BNR) efforts and make for a better industry.

The concept of large Property risks has been established and agreed that large risks
should be classified and minimally rated based on international conventions and

b) LIABILITIES
The concept of large Liability exposures has also been established and agreed that such

conventions and parameters for the classification and rating of liability risks whilst also
taking cognizance of peculiar nature of risks as well as previous loss experience
The concept of Frequency and Severity may help to put such exposures in perspective
when pricing such risks.
TREATMENT OF LARGE RISKS
ASSAR Technical Committee recommended that when a large risk as defined is not
covered by reinsurance treaty, put simply large risk is a risk that exceeds an insurer’s
underwriting capacity (underwriting capacity: net retention + treaty capacity), such risk
should be passed on to ASSAR Technical Pool for proper Management and Placement.
Respective insurers who are interested in the risk can share proportions amongst
members of the Pool until market capacity is fully exhausted and any Excess
proportions of the risk can then be placed offshore by the lead insurer who introduced
the risk to the pool.
Such placement of the risk amongst members of the pool would be in the form of an
Internal Co-insurance possibly not known to the insured. It is basically a means to fully
exhaust market capacity and defeat fronting as well as retain a good quantum of the
premium in the market rather than allow such premiums to drift off into foreign
markets.
It is also believe that such processes will help to grow the market and foster more
bonding amongst insurers in the market and also cub capital flight.
ASSAR Technical Pool currently exist to oversee the management of such large risks

CO-INSURANCE ARRANGEMENT
ASSAR Technical Committee agreed that when large risks are co-insured; co-insurers
must follow the lead insurer.
Large risks are determined by the Sum Insured. Also, the Committee emphasized that
each insurer should be responsible for managing its own proportional premiums as
well as proportional claims based on its share of the large risk
All companies on co-insurance arrangement should present respective companies’
Cheques to the lead insurer through ASSAR Technical Committee / Pool.
The arrangement should be internal and the client should not know that Cheques are
being consolidated for the payment of claims.
Where one of the co-insurers default in fulfilling its payment obligation, the Technical
Committee will meet and discuss the matter and where the concerned co-insurer is not
meeting up her obligations; the case should be reported immediately through ASSAR
to BNR.
On underwriting issues, ASSAR Technical Committee agreed that Heads of
Underwriting Departments may join their Office representatives on the Technical
committee / Pool to discuss how best to manage any large risks that is to be placed in
the market.

POLICY EXCESS
Policy Excess is an amount agreed between the insurer and the insured which must be
deducted from the insured’s claim whenever a claim is made.
The main reason for an excess is to avoid insurers’ being engaged on trivial claims and
consequently save the insurer’s administration costs.
Policy Excess is agreed and insured bears all cost and takes care of all losses / damages
below the Policy Excess Amount. This practice is international and operates in all
insurance markets
In Motor Insurance, Policy Excess encourages good and careful driving by insured's who
know that minor loss / damages are not covered under the policy.
POLICY DEDUCTIBLE:
Policy Deductible is an amount selected by the insured from the onset of the Policy and
below which the insured wishes to bear all losses which fall below the deductible
amount.
Policy Deductible is a large excess usually retained by the insured on large risks. ASSAR
Technical Committee has agreed a minimum mandatory deductibles/excesses for all
risks covers with a possibility of adding voluntary deductibles/excesses on top of
mandatory ones. These voluntary excesses/deductibles may apply to any kind of risk.

POLICY FRANCHISE
Policy Franchise is an amount or time agreed between the insurer and the insured and
any adjusted loss falling below the Franchise amount or time must be borne by the
insured whilst adjusted losses above the Franchise amount or time must be paid in full
inclusive of amounts below the Franchise amount or time subject to indemnity and
Policy Limits.
COVER LIMITS
Cover limits are used mostly for Special Perils and they indicate the limit of insurer’s
exposure on that particular Special Peril.
For example: a limit may be placed on the Special Peril for Flood and Water damages
as Rwf500,000. This means that on no account will insurer pay for Flood or Water
damage losses above Rwf500,000 on that policy.
POLICY FEES
It is agreed that minimum policy fees/ administrative fees should be RWF 5,000 net of
Taxes. This fee is applicable for all new business, renewal business and policy
amendments.

PRICING OF BURGLARY AND THEFT
INSURANCE
The cover on Burglary and Theft that insurers are willing to provide comes in 3 different
wordings depending on the one that the insurer is willing to provide based on the nature
and circumstance of the items to be insured.
None of the wordings align with the definition of Theft as provided by the Theft Act of
1968 and which defines Theft as follows: "A person is guilty of Theft if he dishonestly
appropriates property belonging to another with the intention of permanently depriving
the other of it and thief and steal shall be construed accordingly" Theft Act of 1968
The above definition constitutes LACENY and insurers are usually not willing to grant
such wide covers since it does not leave the insurer with any chances of safeguarding
its portfolio.
Therefore, insurers are only willing to provide Theft and Burglary covers on any of the
following wordings:
1. Theft accompanied by forcible and violent Entry or Exit from insured
premises
2. Theft accompanied by Forcible and violent Entry into insured premises
3. Theft accompanied by Forcible and violent Entry and Forcible and violent
Exit from insured premises
Common Wording used:
The policy indemnifies the insured for loss of or damage to properties resulting from theft
or attempt thereat following visible and violent entry or exit from the premises.
The policy also covers damage to building or property in the bid to effect entry or exit
in connection with the Burglary or Theft

Political Violence and Terrorism often result in huge losses particularly when sensitive
installations and High valued Assets are targeted for destruction.

caused by political motivations or Terrorism.

investigated and confirmed by the Police.
Any organization to which these covers are granted must have confirmed adequate

liability.

conditions and retain only as little as their Accounts can carry.

Capital unless where the insurer was able to secure Reinsurance for the risk.
